title: Digital Evolution
---

Digital evolution studies populations of self-replicating computational entities
that can vary, reproduce, and evolve under selection.

## Why it matters here

Avida-ED uses digital evolution as a teaching environment. It gives students a
way to observe evolutionary change in a system that is fast enough to explore in
class and structured enough to support hypothesis-driven work.

The legacy project materials also stressed that Avida-ED is intended to support
teaching about both evolution and the nature of science. Students are not just
watching a model run; they are expected to form questions, test ideas, and use
evidence from repeated runs.

## Connections to biology education

Digital evolution can help learners examine:

- mutation as a source of heritable variation
- selection acting on differences among organisms
- adaptation emerging over many generations
- the relationship between evidence, explanation, and experimental design

## Educational value

For classroom use, the strength of digital evolution is not that it replaces
biological examples. Its strength is that it creates a manageable experimental
setting where students can test ideas and then connect those observations back
to broader evolutionary concepts.
