This page is the learner-facing starting point for Avida-ED.

The deeper [student support section](/docs/students) now lives in the docs area
so that students can move through a stable sequence of
[launch help](/app), [first steps](/docs/students/first-steps),
[FAQs](/docs/students/faq), and [video transcripts](/docs/videos).

## Start here

- Launch the current app from the [Launch Avida-ED](/app) page.
- Watch the overview video on the home page if you need a quick introduction.
- Use your course instructions to identify which exercise, lesson, or project
  your instructor assigned, such as the
  [lab manual](/curriculum/lab-manual) or [model lessons](/curriculum/model-lessons).
- Continue to the full [student materials guide](/docs/students) for workflow
  help, FAQs, and transcript-first video support.

## What you use Avida-ED for

Students use Avida-ED to:

- observe how digital organisms change over time
- test ideas about mutation, variation, selection, and adaptation
- compare predictions with evidence gathered from experiments
- discuss how experimental design affects the conclusions you can draw

## If you are new to the software

Begin with a short orientation:

1. Launch [Avida-ED 4](/app).
2. Identify the experiment or lesson assigned in your course.
3. Record observations as you work rather than relying on memory afterward.
4. Save or export what your instructor requests for class discussion or
   submission.

## Need help?

- If the app does not open, return to [Launch Avida-ED](/app).
- If you need course materials, ask your instructor which lab or model lesson
  you should use, or review [Curriculum](/curriculum).
- If you need help completing an assignment, use [For Students](/docs/students).
- If you need background on the software and its educational purpose, see
  [What Is Avida-ED?](/about/avida-ed)
